...Welcome...

The world is full of monsters. Terrible creatures that lurk in every shadow. Hidden behind every unknown corner. Waiting for the unaware.

But not all monsters wear monster's skin...

The silent, snowy landscape seems to stretch for miles. The forest creaks and groans, mighty branches laden with frost. A distant rumble carries with it the sound of danger and death. Black cloaks writhe in the whipping wind, five silent figures a dark blotch in a sea of blinding white. One crouches amidst the swirling ice shards, fingers ghosting over trampled snow. The trail of the monsters is clear, but faintly there is another path.

The trees grow thicker as the monster tracks lead deeper and deeper into the forest. An icy fog descends. The once distant rumble grows ever louder. Nearby a branch snaps loudly under the weight of something large.

The undergrowth rustles violently. A momentary glimpse of a wildcat as a massive form barrels through the trees. It yowls in alarm before a vicious black maw snaps closed. All at once there is noise. A deep rumble that vibrates the very bones. Whistles of alarm from companions ahead. The awful thing turns, lets out its terrible roar, and lunges.

Massive claws slam down into the once occupied snow. Another monster sails overhead colliding with its companion. They snarl and snap at each other and it is plenty enough distraction to bring the blade down upon them. There are more, eleven in all, that the group makes quick work of. Nothing left now but to burn the remains. The monsters don't like to stay dead after all.

The sword forms easily. Massive claws collide violently with it. Stopped mere inches away from striking flesh. There is a rumble to the right and the sword twists channeling momentum to send the monster tumbling into its companions path. They fall in a tangle of snarling, snapping, and flailing limbs. Sharp teeth miss by inches. But another monster leaps, colliding in midair. There is no memory of hitting the ground.

The undergrowth rustles violently. A yowl of alarm cut short. All at once there is noise. A deep rumble that vibrates the very bones. Blistering darkness descends- hot, wet, and red.

By the time they make it back to the clearing the sun has begun to set. The faint tracks have long since been filled in with fresh snowfall.

It's easy to slip away unnoticed, silence as easy as breathing. Each step ghostlike across the frozen ground. The trees begin to thin, interspersed with clusters of brambles. There is a path here, narrow and well worn.

The path continues, gentle and winding. When the forest clears there is a house. It sits small but proud, made of rough logs that seem to lean inward on each other. Smoke puffs steadily from the chimney, a candleflame wavers cheerfully in the window. It looks cozy. Something feels off.

Immediately there is the feeling of being watched. Approaching the house evidence that something is wrong becomes more apparent. A clay mug sits abandoned near an ax that lies haphazardly in the snow. There is still a faint warmth from it, rapidly being leached away. The door sits ever so slightly ajar. The candleflame flickers, and dies. Light glints off something bright and yellow in the woods. A black shadow crouches among the trees. A second flits by at the edge of vision.

The black clad figure lunges. A knife forms easily in their grip and bites deep into flesh. The bright, cheery, sunshine-yellow of their mask is utterly impassive. An alarm whistle pierces the silence, but it will be answered far too late.

Snow slips underfoot as clawed feet scramble for purchase. There is danger here. Too risky to be faced alone. The path is narrow, so narrow. Branches and brambles tug insistently, each frozen snap breaks the silence like a siren wail.

It's not hard to find the rest of the team. The last dying bellows of the monsters echo through the forest. Eleven monsters are strewn about. A warning whistle too late as a knife streaks through the air. A twelfth body joins them, knife buried in the neck of an amber-masked companion. The fight is swift and brutal. Soon yellow, red, and blue too join the rest on the forest floor. It is not a victory today.

There is a new tension in the air as the mysterious tracks are observed. They are too small for monsters. Too carful for the locals. The only conclusion is the worst.

The group reconvenes and approaches cautiously. The shadow in the trees doesn't move. Something else does. Black-clad figure, red mask like blood, battle-ax bites furiously into the frozen ground. Suddenly the yellow masked figure lunges from the other's shadow, knife at the ready. It's over quickly. Two against five was never likely to succeed.
