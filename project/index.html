...Welcome...

The world is full of monsters...

And not all monsters wear monster's skin...

The silent, snowy landscape seems to stretch for miles. The forest creaks and groans, mighty branches laden with frost. A distant rumble carries with it the sound of danger and death. Black cloaks writhe in the whipping wind, five silent figures a dark blotch in a sea of blinding white. One crouches amidst the swirling ice shards, fingers ghosting over trampled snow. The trail of the monsters is clear, but faintly there is another path.

The trees grow thicker as the tracks lead deeper and deeper into the forest. An icy fog descends. The once distant rumble grows ever louder. Nearby a branch snaps loudly under the weight of something large.

The undergrowth rustles violently. A momentary glimpse of a wildcat as a massive form barrels through the trees. It yowls in alarm before a viscious black maw snaps closed. All at once there is noise. A deep rumble that vibrates the very bones. Whistles of alarm from companions ahead. The awful thing turns, lets out its terrible roar, and lunges.

The undergrowth rustles violently. A yown of alarm cut short. All at once there is noise. A deep rumble that vibrates the very bones. Blistering darkness descends- hot, wet, and red.

It's easy to slip away unnoticed, silence as easy as breathing. Each step ghostlike across the frozen ground. The trees begin to thin, interspersed with clusters of brambles. There is a path here, narrow and well worn.

The path continues, gentle and winding. When the forest clears there is a house. It sits small but proud, made of rough logs that seem to lean inward on eachother. Smoke puffs steadily from the chimney, a candleflame wavers cheerfully in the window. It looks cozy. But something feels off.

There is a new tension in the air as the mysterious tracks are observed. They are too small for monsters. Too carful for the locals. The only conclusion is the worst.
